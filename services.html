chit-chat & laughs

Upbeat and lively conversations are a mental health pick me up for everyone involved.

out & about

Take them on a virtual stroll on the beach, or to their favorite park or part of town. Share the sights virtually right on the spot.

music & games

It's not only fun, it can improve their quality of life. Listening to music together, talking about songs, nostalgia, and playing games keeps the mind sharp and helps boost memory.

groceries & medicine

Trips to the grocery store and pharmacy are essential. Running these errands for the most vulnerable could save their lives.

letters & care packages

Junk mail is depressing. Actual correspondence is exciting, especially for someone stuck at home for months on end.

DIY crafts & gardening

Think of this as a MasterClass of videos to help guide them to create things they can use or give as gifts. For each lesson, they will have the opportunity to order the supplies needed to complete the project ahead of time.
