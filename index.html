Why we do it

The most vulnerable among us are the ones who paved the way for us. We can never forget that. During this pandemic, there are grave risks to physical isolation. With stay at home orders going on almost a year, the older generation is at risk for depression, and heart ailments, and loneliness. They deserve our time and effort to be there for their needs.

Comfort in Crisis

Social contact impacts our mental and physical health. The 2020 pandemic left us homebound, and in a way, left some helpless. While we have been physically forced apart and shut in, this is how many elderly people felt even before the restrictions of COVID-19. Can you imagine how they feel now?


The mission

We have to stay connected and build genuine friendships with the people in our communities. Reach out to those who are shut in. Identify who needs help, or companionship, or comfort. Assist them to the best of our abilities.
